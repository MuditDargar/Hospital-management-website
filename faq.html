FAQ

What do we do?

healthcare professionals provide medical care to patients who are sick, injured, or in need of medical attention. They diagnose and treat a variety of medical conditions, perform surgeries, administer medications, and provide other medical treatments. Hospitals also offer support services such as laboratory testing, imaging, and rehabilitation services. Additionally, hospitals focus on preventing the spread of infections and promoting patient safety by implementing strict protocols and procedures.

How do we do it?

Hospitals operate through the coordinated efforts of healthcare professionals and support staff who work together to provide quality medical care to patients. This involves following established protocols and procedures for patient care, including conducting medical assessments, administering medications and treatments, and monitoring patient progress.

When do we work?

Hospital work can involve a range of professions and roles, such as doctors, nurses, administrative staff, pharmacists, laboratory technicians, and janitorial staff. They may work in various departments, such as emergency, intensive care, surgery, and pediatrics. Hospital work typically involves long and demanding hours, involving caring for patients, administering treatments, documenting medical records, collaborating with other healthcare professionals, and maintaining a clean and safe environment.

How competent are our staff?

The competency of hospital staff is critical to ensuring quality patient care. Competence is achieved through a combination of education, training, and experience. Hospitals typically have a rigorous recruitment process, ongoing training programs, and performance evaluations to maintain staff competence.
